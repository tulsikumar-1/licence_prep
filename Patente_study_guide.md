## 1. How the exam is scored, and the one habit that wins

Each real Patente B quiz item is a sentence you mark **V** or **F**. There is no separate question stem — the sentence *is* the claim. That means the exam is largely a test of **how carefully a claim is worded**. The single most powerful habit: when a sentence makes the world too tidy (*only*, *always*, *never*, *exclusively*, *in any case*, *all X are*), suspect **FALSO**; when it is cautious and conditional (*it is advisable*, *unless*, *in the presence of*, *before*), lean **VERO**. The tables below quantify exactly how reliable each of those tells is.

## 3. TRUE-answer patterns

**Safe-driving advice is true.** Statements telling you to slow down, increase distance, be cautious, or check before acting are almost always correct.
  *Signal:* `è consigliabile` 78% T, `è opportuno` 66% T, `prima di` 66% T

**Conditional duties (when X, do Y).** Rules phrased with an explicit condition mirror the Codice della Strada and are usually true.
  *Signal:* `in presenza di` 73% T, `salvo` 72% T

**Bad weather increases danger.** Ice, rain, snow, fog → slower speed, longer braking, less grip. True almost every time.
  *Signal:* `ghiaccio` 71% T, `pioggia` 62% T, `neve` 58% T

**Caution at junctions.** Approaching crossroads/intersections demands extra care and often yielding.
  *Signal:* `incroci` 67% T, `intersezione` 58% T

**Documents, belts, helmet, inspection are required.** Statements asserting a legal obligation to carry documents, wear belts/helmet, insure and inspect the vehicle are generally true.

Worked examples from the listing (all labelled **VERO**):
- In presenza del segnale raffigurato il conducente, salvo diverso ulteriore segnale, deve dare precedenza ai veicoli pro­ venienti da destra
- Il segnale raffigurato vieta la sosta sulle strade urbane dalle ore 8.00 alle ore 20.00, salvo diversa indicazione
- Il marciapiede consente la sosta dei veicoli solo in presenza di apposite strisce di parcheggio
- Il segnale raffigurato, se a fondo giallo, è usato in presenza di cantieri stradali
- Il segnale (A), se abbinato con il pannello (B), preannuncia un tratto di strada sul quale, in particolari condizioni meteo­ rologiche, è probabile la presenza di ghiaccio (FIG 917)

## 4. FALSE-answer patterns & traps

**The absolute trap.** A sweeping word removes the exception that the real rule has.
  *Signal:* `mai` 8% T · `esclusivamente` 7% T · `in ogni caso` 10% T · `solo` 25% T · `sempre` 35% T

**The over-broad definition.** “All X are Y” / “only X is Y” classifications are usually wrong because categories overlap.
  *Signal:* `tutti … sono` 36% T

**The dangerous-action trap.** Claims that you may overtake near bends/humps/crossings/level-crossings, speed up in bad weather, or park on hazard lights are false.

**The reversed-physics trap.** “Stopping distance is shorter on wet roads”, “tyres grip better in rain” — false; the opposite holds.

**The priority trap.** “Priority always belongs to the bigger / faster vehicle” — false; priority follows signs, signals and the right-hand rule.

**The optional-safety trap.** “Belts/helmet optional for short trips / in town”, “insurance unnecessary” — false.

Worked examples from the listing (all labelled **FALSO**):
- La corsia non può mai essere attraversata dai pedoni
- I caravan (roulottes) sono rimorchi destinati esclusivamente al trasporto di attrezzature turistiche e sportive
- Il segnale raffigurato indica che in ogni caso bisogna tenersi il più possibile sul margine destro della carreggiata
- Si definiscono ciclomotori tutti i veicoli a due ruote di cilindrata superiore a 50 cm3
- Si definiscono motocicli solo i veicoli a due ruote di cilindrata fino a 125 cm3

## 5. Confusing pairs — one word changes the answer

| Distinction | Meaning | Why it flips the answer |
|---|---|---|
| `fermata` vs `sosta` | brief halt (driver present) vs parking (driver may leave) | A ban on *sosta* can still allow *fermata*. |
| `in prossimità` vs `in corrispondenza` | approaching/near vs exactly at | Overtaking and stopping bans attach to one or the other, not both. |
| `può` vs `deve` | may (permission) vs must (duty) | Turning a permission into a duty (or vice-versa) makes a true rule false. |
| `consentito` vs `vietato` | permitted vs forbidden | Same scene, opposite verb — answer depends on road type. |
| `obbligo` vs `divieto` | obligation vs prohibition | A blue obligation sign ≠ a red prohibition sign. |
| `centro abitato` vs `fuori` | inside vs outside built-up area | Speed, horn and lighting rules differ; an urban truth is often extra-urban false. |
| `urbana` vs `extraurbana` vs `autostrada` | road classes | Each has its own speed limits and permitted vehicles. |
| `sorpasso` vs `superamento` | overtaking vs passing/exceeding | Different rules; don't read one as the other. |
| `curva` vs `dosso` | bend vs hump/crest | Both restrict overtaking, but conditions differ by road layout. |

Real contrast from the listing — overtaking near a hump (`dosso`):
- **VERO** — Il sorpasso in prossimità di un dosso è consentito sulle strade a senso unico
- **VERO** — Il sorpasso in prossimità di un dosso è consentito sulle carreggiate a senso unico
- **FALSO** — Il segnale raffigurato preannuncia un dosso
- **FALSO** — Il segnale raffigurato preannuncia una cunetta o un dosso

*Why:* overtaking near a hump is forbidden on ordinary two-way two-lane roads, but permitted where the carriageway is one-way, or has separated carriageways with ≥2 lanes each way.

## 8. One-screen cheat sheet

**Lean FALSE:** `mai`, `esclusivamente`, `in ogni caso`, `solo`/`soltanto`, `sempre`, `tutti … sono`.

**Lean TRUE:** `è consigliabile`/`è opportuno`, `salvo`, `in presenza di`, `ghiaccio`/`pioggia`/`neve`, `incroci`, `prima di`, `entro`.

**Depends — read the rule:** `deve`/`può`, `consentito`/`vietato`, `obbligo`/`divieto`, `non`, `precedenza`, `velocità`, `sorpasso`, `carreggiata`/`corsia`.

**Always-true safe driving:** slow near danger · yield to pedestrians · keep (and extend) safety distance · mirrors + signal before manoeuvres · never drive impaired · stop & help after a crash · belts/helmet/child seat required · obey police over signs.

**Always-false traps:** overtake near bend/hump/crossing/level-crossing · speed up in fog/rain/ice · park on hazard lights · shorter braking on wet roads · belts optional in town · priority to the bigger/faster vehicle · ignore a sign when the road looks clear.

**Key distinctions:** fermata≠sosta · in prossimità≠in corrispondenza · può≠deve · consentito≠vietato · centro abitato≠fuori · urbana≠extraurbana≠autostrada.
