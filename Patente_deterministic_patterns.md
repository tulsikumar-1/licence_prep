# Patente A/B — deterministic patterns & traps

These are the *certainties*, not the leanings. Each item below appears on only one side across all 7,144 statements in the listing, or follows a rule of the road that is always true. Use this together with the percentage tables — the percentages tell you where to be careful, these tell you where you can be sure.

> Honesty flag: items marked **(sign)** depend on a specific sign image, so they mean "when you see this exact wording the answer was X", not a standalone law. Items marked **(rule)** generalise beyond this listing.

## 1. Exact numbers — learn the right one, every other value is the trap

Speeds and masses depend on context, so the listing makes its false twins by swapping the number. Memorise the correct figure; a different number in the same sentence is the wrong answer.

| Topic | Correct value | Trap value used | Example (this one is FALSE) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mopeds (ciclomotori) | **45 km/h max** | 60 km/h | I ciclomotori possono circolare alla velocità di 60 km/h fuori dai centri urbani |
| Light trailer (rimorchio leggero) | **up to 750 kg** | 500 kg | I rimorchi leggeri sono quelli di massa massima a pieno carico fino a 500 kg |
| Motorbike on motorway | **at least 150 cm³** | 125 cm³ | Nelle autostrade possono circolare i ciclomotori e i motocicli con cilindrata fino a 125 cm3 |
| Light quad (quadriciclo leggero) | **≤ 50 cm³ / power limit** | raise speed past 45 | È consentito sostituire il motore del quadriciclo leggero per aumentare la velocità massima oltre i 45 km/h |
| Periodic inspection (revisione) | **after 4 yrs, then every 2 yrs** | every 5 years / every year | Le autovetture sono soggette a revisione ogni cinque anni |

Also watch the **3.5 t** boundary: many car speed-limit rules hinge on whether mass is above or below 3.5 tonnes, and the listing swaps which side you're on.

## 2. Structural traps (not single words)

**A. The narrowing trap — `solo / soltanto / unicamente / esclusivamente` + `quando / se / nelle ore`.** (rule)
A duty restricted to a single condition is almost always false, because the real rule is general. In the listing `dare la precedenza solo…`, `solo quando…`, `unicamente…`, `solo nelle ore…` are all FALSE.

**B. The negated-duty trap — `non è obbligatorio` / `non è necessario` / `non comporta`.** (rule)
Claiming a safety or legal duty is *not* required is false every time it appears (12× / 9× / 11×, never true). If a sentence says you don't have to do something careful, suspect FALSO.

**C. The reduce-the-margin trap — `diminuire la distanza di sicurezza`.** (rule)
Anything that lets you shorten the safety distance, brake harder, or push on where you shouldn't is false. Safety margins only ever grow.

**D. The over-broad definition — `tutti i … sono …` / `si definiscono … tutti …`.** (rule)
Sweeping "all X are Y" classifications are wrong because vehicle and road categories overlap. Only ~36% of `tutti … sono` statements are true.

**E. The danger-sign duty template — `il segnale raffigurato richiede di moderare la velocità / rallentare / usare prudenza`.** (sign)
A warning sign that "requires moderating speed / slowing / caution" is described correctly → TRUE (13×, never false).

**F. The direction-sign template — `il segnale raffigurato preavvisa di svoltare / proseguire …`.** (sign)
Describing what a direction sign pre-announces (turn / continue toward a place) → TRUE (11×, never false).

## 4. Always-true / always-false road rules (generalise to any exam)

**Always TRUE:** slow down near danger · give way to pedestrians at crossings · keep (and extend) the safety distance · mirrors + signal before every manoeuvre · never drive impaired · stop and help after a crash · belts / helmet / child seat required · obey the police over signs and lights.

**Always FALSE:** overtaking near a bend / hump / crossing / level crossing (except one-way or separated carriageways) · speeding up in fog / rain / ice · hazard lights legalising illegal parking · shorter braking on wet roads · belts optional in town · priority to the bigger or faster vehicle · ignoring a sign because the road looks clear.
